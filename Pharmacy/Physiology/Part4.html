121. The primary function of the SA node in the heart is:

  • A. To regulate blood pressure
  • B. To initiate the cardiac impulse
  • C. To control ventricular contraction
  • D. To filter blood entering the heart

Answer: B. To initiate the cardiac impulse - The SA node acts as the natural pacemaker of the heart, generating electrical impulses that initiate each heartbeat.

01:30

122. The Frank-Starling mechanism of the heart states that:

  • A. Heart rate increases with exercise
  • B. Cardiac output increases with increased venous return
  • C. Blood pressure regulates heart contraction
  • D. The heart pumps what it receives

Answer: B. Cardiac output increases with increased venous return - This mechanism describes how the heart automatically adjusts its pumping capacity to match the volume of blood returning to it.

01:30

123. The primary factor that determines blood flow through a vessel is:

  • A. Blood viscosity
  • B. Vessel length
  • C. Pressure gradient
  • D. Vessel radius

Answer: C. Pressure gradient - Blood flows from areas of higher pressure to areas of lower pressure, making the pressure difference the primary driving force.

01:30

124. The primary function of hemoglobin is to:

  • A. Transport oxygen in the blood
  • B. Fight infection
  • C. Aid in blood clotting
  • D. Regulate blood pH

Answer: A. Transport oxygen in the blood - Hemoglobin binds to oxygen in the lungs and releases it in tissues, facilitating oxygen transport throughout the body.

01:30

125. The primary stimulus for breathing in a healthy person is:

  • A. Low oxygen levels in the blood
  • B. High carbon dioxide levels in the blood
  • C. Low blood pressure
  • D. High blood pH

Answer: B. High carbon dioxide levels in the blood - The central chemoreceptors are most sensitive to changes in CO2 levels, which directly affect blood pH.

01:30

126. The primary function of the kidneys is to:

  • A. Regulate fluid and electrolyte balance
  • B. Produce red blood cells
  • C. Digest nutrients
  • D. Produce hormones for growth

Answer: A. Regulate fluid and electrolyte balance - The kidneys maintain homeostasis by filtering blood, reabsorbing needed substances, and excreting waste products.

01:30

127. The cardiac cycle consists of:

  • A. Systole and diastole
  • B. Inspiration and expiration
  • C. Filtration and reabsorption
  • D. Depolarization and repolarization

Answer: A. Systole and diastole - Systole is the contraction phase when blood is ejected, and diastole is the relaxation phase when the heart fills with blood.

01:30

128. The Bohr effect describes:

  • A. How CO2 and H+ affect oxygen binding to hemoglobin
  • B. How oxygen affects CO2 transport
  • C. How blood pressure affects heart rate
  • D. How kidney function affects blood pH

Answer: A. How CO2 and H+ affect oxygen binding to hemoglobin - Increased CO2 and H+ cause hemoglobin to release oxygen more readily in tissues.

129. The glomerular filtration rate (GFR) is primarily regulated by:

  • A. Antidiuretic hormone (ADH)
  • B. Autoregulation and sympathetic nervous system
  • C. Aldosterone
  • D. Atrial natriuretic peptide (ANP)

Answer: B. Autoregulation and sympathetic nervous system - The kidneys autoregulate GFR through myogenic and tubuloglomerular feedback mechanisms.

01:30

130. The Hering-Breuer reflex is involved in:

  • A. Preventing overinflation of the lungs
  • B. Regulating blood pressure
  • C. Controlling heart rate
  • D. Maintaining acid-base balance

Answer: A. Preventing overinflation of the lungs - Stretch receptors in the lungs inhibit inspiration when lung volume becomes too high.

01:30

131. The hormone that primarily regulates water reabsorption in the kidneys is:

  • A. Antidiuretic hormone (ADH)
  • B. Aldosterone
  • C. Renin
  • D. Atrial natriuretic peptide

Answer: A. Antidiuretic hormone (ADH) - ADH increases water permeability in the collecting ducts, allowing more water reabsorption.

132. The P wave in an ECG represents:

  • A. Atrial depolarization
  • B. Ventricular depolarization
  • C. Atrial repolarization
  • D. Ventricular repolarization

Answer: A. Atrial depolarization - The P wave corresponds to the electrical activation of the atria before contraction.

  • A. Gas exchange between air and blood
  • B. Blood filtration
  • C. Hormone production

Answer: A. Gas exchange between air and blood - The respiratory system facilitates oxygen uptake and carbon dioxide elimination.

Physiology Exam Tips

1. Understand Mechanisms, Not Just Facts

Focus on understanding how physiological systems work together rather than memorizing isolated facts. Pay special attention to feedback mechanisms and regulatory pathways.

2. Master Cardiovascular Physiology

The cardiovascular system is heavily tested. Be sure to understand cardiac cycle, ECG interpretation, blood pressure regulation, and hemodynamics.

3. Practice Integrated Systems

Many exam questions test how multiple systems interact (e.g., how respiratory and renal systems work together to regulate acid-base balance).

4. Know Key Equations

Memorize and understand important physiological equations like cardiac output, blood flow, and clearance formulas.

5. Focus on Homeostasis

Physiology is fundamentally about maintaining homeostasis. Understand how each system contributes to this balance and how they respond to challenges.
