Introduction to Pathology

Pathology is the study of disease and the bridge between science and medicine. It underpins every aspect of patient care, from diagnostic testing and treatment advice to using cutting-edge genetic technologies [citation:5].

Key Pathology Concepts:

  • Etiology: The cause of disease (genetic, infectious, environmental)
  • Pathogenesis: The mechanism of disease development
  • Morphologic changes: Structural alterations in cells and tissues
  • Clinical manifestations: Signs and symptoms of disease

Anatomical Pathology

Focuses on examining organs, tissues, and whole bodies to diagnose disease. Includes surgical pathology, cytopathology, and forensic pathology [citation:10].

Clinical Pathology

Diagnosis of disease through laboratory analysis of bodily fluids and tissues. Includes hematology, microbiology, and chemical pathology [citation:7].

Molecular Pathology

Examines molecules within organs, tissues or bodily fluids. Leads to personalized medicine through genetic analysis and predictive biomarkers [citation:7].
