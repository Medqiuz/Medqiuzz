Chapter 1: Fundamentals of Measurement and Calculations

1
How many significant figures are in the measurement 0.05060 g?
A) 3
B) 4
C) 5
D) 6
Correct Answer: B) 4
Explanation: The zeros before 5 are not significant (they only show decimal placement), but the zero between 5 and 6 is significant, and the final zero after 6 is also significant. So we have: 5, 0, 6, 0 = 4 significant figures.
2
A Class A prescription balance has a sensitivity requirement of 6 mg. What is the minimum amount that should be weighed to keep the percentage error at or below 5%?
A) 60 mg
B) 100 mg
C) 120 mg
D) 150 mg
Correct Answer: C) 120 mg
Explanation: Minimum weighable amount = (Sensitivity requirement × 100) / Maximum % error = (6 mg × 100) / 5 = 600/5 = 120 mg.
3
A pharmacist measured 25 mL of a liquid using a 100-mL cylindrical graduate. If there was a 1 mm error in reading the meniscus, approximately what volume error would this represent?
A) 0.125 mL
B) 0.25 mL
C) 0.5 mL
D) 1.0 mL
Correct Answer: A) 0.125 mL
Explanation: According to USP, ±1 mm meniscus reading error causes ~0.5 mL error in a 100-mL cylindrical graduate. Since we're measuring 25 mL (1/4 of 100 mL), the error would be approximately 0.5 mL ÷ 4 = 0.125 mL.
4
If 50 mL of glycerin weighs 62.5 g, what is its specific gravity?
A) 0.8
B) 1.0
C) 1.25
D) 1.5
Correct Answer: C) 1.25
Explanation: Specific gravity = Weight of substance / Weight of equal volume of water. 50 mL of water weighs 50 g. So sp gr = 62.5 g / 50 g = 1.25.
5
What is the specific volume of a liquid with a specific gravity of 0.8?
A) 0.8 mL/g
B) 1.0 mL/g
C) 1.25 mL/g
D) 1.5 mL/g
Correct Answer: C) 1.25 mL/g
Explanation: Specific volume and specific gravity are reciprocals. Specific volume = 1 / Specific gravity = 1 / 0.8 = 1.25 mL/g.
6
A pharmacist needs to weigh 10 mg of a potent drug using a balance with a sensitivity requirement of 5 mg. Using the aliquot method with lactose as diluent and a multiple of 20, what total weight of drug-diluent mixture should be prepared if the aliquot portion is set at 150 mg?
A) 1,000 mg
B) 2,000 mg
C) 3,000 mg
D) 4,000 mg
Correct Answer: C) 3,000 mg
Explanation: Multiple quantity = 10 mg × 20 = 200 mg. Aliquot portion = 150 mg. Total mixture = Aliquot portion × Multiple = 150 mg × 20 = 3,000 mg.
7
If a 250 mL volumetric flask weighs 150 g when empty and 400 g when filled with water, what is the volume of the flask?
A) 200 mL
B) 250 mL
C) 300 mL
D) 350 mL
Correct Answer: B) 250 mL
Explanation: Weight of water = 400 g - 150 g = 250 g. Since density of water is 1 g/mL, volume = 250 mL.
8
Which of the following measurements has the greatest number of significant figures?
A) 0.0050 g
B) 5.00 g
C) 500 g
D) 0.500 g
Correct Answer: B) 5.00 g
Explanation: A) 0.0050 g has 2 significant figures; B) 5.00 g has 3; C) 500 g has 1 (trailing zeros without decimal are not significant); D) 0.500 g has 3. Both B and D have 3, but the question asks for the greatest number.
9
A pycnometer weighs 25.50 g when empty, 75.60 g when filled with water, and 80.10 g when filled with an unknown liquid. What is the specific gravity of the unknown liquid?
A) 0.90
B) 1.09
C) 1.14
D) 1.25
Correct Answer: B) 1.09
Explanation: Weight of water = 75.60 - 25.50 = 50.10 g. Weight of liquid = 80.10 - 25.50 = 54.60 g. Specific gravity = 54.60 / 50.10 = 1.09.
10
When using the aliquot method to measure 0.8 mL of a liquid with a 10-mL graduate calibrated in 1-mL divisions, which multiple would be most appropriate?
A) 2
B) 4
C) 5
D) 10
Correct Answer: C) 5
Explanation: With a 10-mL graduate, measuring less than 2 mL is inaccurate. Using a multiple of 5: 0.8 mL × 5 = 4 mL, which can be measured accurately. Then dilute to a measurable volume and take 1/5 aliquot.

Chapter 3: Calculations of Doses and Formulation

If the adult dose of a drug is 500 mg, what would be the approximate dose for a child with a body surface area of 0.65 m²? (Average adult BSA = 1.73 m²)

B) 188 mg

D) 375 mg
Correct Answer: B) 188 mg
Explanation: Child dose = (Child's BSA / Adult BSA) × Adult dose = (0.65 / 1.73) × 500 mg = 0.376 × 500 = 188 mg.

1
A prescription balance has a sensitivity requirement of 6 mg. Explain how you would weigh 15 mg of atropine sulfate with an error not greater than 5%, using lactose as the diluent.

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate minimum weighable amount:

Minimum weight = (Sensitivity × 100) / % error = (6 mg × 100) / 5 = 120 mg

Step 2: Select a multiple so that multiple × 15 mg ≥ 120 mg

120 mg ÷ 15 mg = 8, so use multiple of 8 (or higher)

If multiple = 8: Weigh 15 mg × 8 = 120 mg of atropine sulfate

Step 3: Dilute with lactose. If we set aliquot portion = 150 mg (≥ 120 mg):

Total mixture = Aliquot × Multiple = 150 mg × 8 = 1200 mg

Lactose needed = 1200 mg - 120 mg = 1080 mg

Step 4: Weigh 150 mg of the mixture (contains 15 mg atropine sulfate)
