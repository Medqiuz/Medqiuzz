Sarcodina (Amoebae) - Medical Protozoology Examination

Conceptual

1. Sarcodina is a subphylum characterized by movement using:
Sarcodina (amoebae) move using pseudopodia (temporary cytoplasmic extensions).

Conceptual

2. Which of the following best describes the nutritional mode of Sarcodina?
Sarcodina exhibit holozoic nutrition - they ingest food particles, including bacteria and host cells.

Contextual

3. A patient presents with chronic diarrhea. Stool examination reveals motile organisms with ingested RBCs. Which subphylum does this organism belong to?
Ingested RBCs in motile organisms are characteristic of Entamoeba histolytica, which belongs to Sarcodina.

Conceptual

4. Which of the following is NOT a common habitat for pathogenic Sarcodina?
While Sarcodina can infect intestines, liver, brain, and eyes, they are not typically found in pulmonary alveoli as primary pathogens.

Contextual

5. Which characteristic structure allows amoebae to move and capture food?
Pseudopodia are temporary cytoplasmic extensions used for both movement (creeping) and food capture (phagocytosis).

Conceptual

6. Which of the following is the primary pathogenic intestinal amoeba?
Entamoeba histolytica is the primary pathogenic species causing amoebic dysentery and extraintestinal infections.

Contextual

7. A laboratory technician examines a stool sample and observes motile organisms with ingested red blood cells. Which stage of E. histolytica is this?
Trophozoites are the motile, feeding stage and the presence of ingested RBCs is diagnostic for E. histolytica.

Conceptual

8. How many nuclei are found in a mature Entamoeba histolytica cyst?
Mature E. histolytica cysts are tetranucleated (4 nuclei), which is the infective stage.

Contextual

9. A traveler returns from a tropical region with bloody diarrhea. Which form of E. histolytica would most likely be found in their diarrheic stool?
In acute dysentery, motile trophozoites with ingested RBCs are found in liquid stools. Cysts are found in formed stools of carriers.

Conceptual

10. What is the primary mode of transmission for Entamoeba histolytica?
E. histolytica is transmitted via the fecal-oral route through ingestion of mature (tetranucleated) cysts.

Conceptual

11. Which virulence factor of E. histolytica is primarily responsible for adherence to colonic epithelial cells?
Gal/GalNAc lectin acts as an adhesin, helping trophozoites adhere to colonic epithelial cells and preventing expulsion by peristalsis.

Contextual

12. A patient with amoebic dysentery develops a liver abscess. How do trophozoites reach the liver?
Trophozoites invade intestinal blood vessels and are carried to the liver via the portal venous system.

Conceptual

13. What is the characteristic shape of intestinal ulcers caused by E. histolytica?
Amoebic ulcers are flask-shaped with narrow neck at mucosal surface and broad base extending into submucosa.

Contextual

14. Which virulence factor of E. histolytica directly lyses host cells by forming pores in their membranes?
Amoebapores are pore-forming peptides that insert into host cell membranes, causing cell lysis and direct cytotoxicity.

Conceptual

15. Which of the following is a key diagnostic feature that differentiates pathogenic E. histolytica from non-pathogenic E. dispar?
E. histolytica trophozoites ingest RBCs (erythrophagocytosis), while non-pathogenic E. dispar does not.

Contextual

16. A patient presents with right upper quadrant pain, fever, and tender hepatomegaly. Ultrasound reveals a solitary lesion in the right lobe of liver with "chocolate-colored" aspirate. What is the most likely diagnosis?
Amoebic liver abscess typically presents with right upper quadrant pain, fever, and tender hepatomegaly. The abscess contains chocolate-colored necrotic material (not true pus).

Conceptual

17. Which lobe of the liver is most commonly affected in amoebic liver abscess?
The right lobe of the liver is most commonly affected (80-90% of cases), possibly due to streaming of portal blood flow.

Contextual

18. A patient with suspected amoebic liver abscess has stool examinations negative for E. histolytica. What explains this finding?
In extraintestinal amoebiasis (like liver abscess), stools are often negative for E. histolytica as the infection is localized outside the intestine.

Conceptual

19. What is the first-line treatment for invasive amoebiasis (colitis or liver abscess)?
Metronidazole (or tinidazole) is the tissue amebicide for invasive disease, followed by a luminal agent to eradicate intestinal cysts.

Contextual

20. Which diagnostic method would be most appropriate for confirming extraintestinal amoebiasis when stool examination is negative?
Serological tests (antibody detection) are highly sensitive for extraintestinal amoebiasis and show high antibody titers even when stool exams are negative.

Conceptual

21. Which non-pathogenic amoeba resides in the oral cavity and is associated with poor dental hygiene?
Entamoeba gingivalis is found in the oral cavity, particularly in gingival pockets and tonsillar crypts, associated with poor oral hygiene.

Contextual

22. A stool examination reveals cysts with 8 nuclei. Which non-pathogenic amoeba is most likely?
Entamoeba coli mature cysts typically have 8 nuclei (sometimes up to 16), compared to E. histolytica which has 4 nuclei.

Conceptual

23. Which non-pathogenic intestinal amoeba is morphologically identical to E. histolytica but does not ingest RBCs?
Entamoeba dispar is morphologically identical to E. histolytica but is non-pathogenic and does not ingest red blood cells.

Contextual

24. A cyst with a large glycogen vacuole that stains brown with iodine is characteristic of:
Iodamoeba buetschlii cysts contain a large glycogen vacuole that stains brown with iodine, giving the parasite its name.

Conceptual

25. Which non-pathogenic amoeba is the smallest intestinal amoeba?
Endolimax nana is the smallest intestinal amoeba, with trophozoites measuring 6-12 μm and cysts 5-10 μm.

Conceptual

26. Which free-living amoeba causes primary amoebic meningoencephalitis (PAM), often fatal within days?
Naegleria fowleri causes rapidly fatal primary amoebic meningoencephalitis (PAM), typically in healthy young individuals after swimming in warm fresh water.

Contextual

27. A healthy young man develops sudden severe headache, fever, vomiting, and stiff neck 5 days after swimming in a warm lake. CSF shows motile amoebae. What is the most likely pathogen?
This is classic presentation of primary amoebic meningoencephalitis (PAM) caused by Naegleria fowleri, with rapid onset after swimming in warm fresh water.

Conceptual

28. Which free-living amoeba causes granulomatous amoebic encephalitis (GAE) and amoebic keratitis?
Acanthamoeba species cause granulomatous amoebic encephalitis (GAE) in immunocompromised individuals and amoebic keratitis in contact lens wearers.

Contextual

29. A contact lens wearer presents with severe eye pain, photophobia, and corneal ulceration. Which amoeba should be suspected?
Acanthamoeba keratitis is associated with contact lens use, especially with improper cleaning or use of tap water for rinsing.

Conceptual

30. What is the unique characteristic of Naegleria that distinguishes it from other amoebae?
Naegleria can exist in three forms: trophozoite (amoeboid), flagellate, and cyst. The transformation to flagellate form in water is unique among pathogenic amoebae.

Conceptual

31. Which virulence factor of E. histolytica degrades extracellular matrix and mucus layer, facilitating tissue invasion?
Cysteine proteinases degrade the mucus layer, extracellular matrix components (collagen, fibronectin), and immunoglobulins, facilitating tissue invasion.

Contextual

32. A patient with amoebic liver abscess develops chest pain and cough. What is the most likely complication?
Amoebic liver abscess, especially in the right lobe, can rupture through the diaphragm causing pleuropulmonary amoebiasis with cough, chest pain, and dyspnea.

Conceptual

33. What is "ameboma" in intestinal amoebiasis?
Ameboma is a rare complication where chronic inflammation causes granulomatous thickening of the intestinal wall, which can mimic colonic cancer on imaging.

Contextual

34. Why are E. histolytica cysts resistant to normal water treatment methods?
Mature cysts have a thick wall that allows survival in the environment: resistant to normal chlorine concentrations, gastric acid, and temperatures up to 55°C.

Conceptual

35. Which of the following statements about pathogenic vs non-pathogenic intestinal amoebae is CORRECT?
E. histolytica is distinguished from non-pathogenic intestinal amoebae by its ability to ingest red blood cells (erythrophagocytosis).

Contextual

36. A patient returns from a tropical country with asymptomatic E. histolytica cysts in stool. What is the appropriate management?
Asymptomatic cyst passers should be treated with luminal amebicides to eliminate intestinal infection and prevent transmission, even if asymptomatic.
